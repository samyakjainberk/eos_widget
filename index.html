Progressive Sharpening & Edge of Stability

An interactive companion to Understanding Progressive Sharpening — full-batch gradient descent on a small network, with every panel tied to the paper's equations · build: theory-v5

Full-batch GD at rate $\eta$ on $f(x)\in\mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{out}}}$. Sharpness $\sigma_1=\lambda_{\max}(\nabla^2_\theta\mathcal L)$ rises (progressive sharpening) until it pins near $2/\eta$ at the edge of stability, where the loss oscillates with period 2.

Following Understanding Progressive Sharpening, over a window we freeze $J_{\theta_t}=\tfrac{df}{d\theta}$ and the function Hessian $Q_{\theta_t}=\tfrac{d^2f}{d\theta^2}$ ($Q_{\theta_{t+\Delta t}}=Q_{\theta_t}$) and predict from the Taylor surrogate

$$f_{\theta_{t+\Delta t}}=f_{\theta_t}+J_{\theta_t}^{\top}\Delta\theta+\Delta\theta^{\top}Q_{\theta_t}\Delta\theta .$$

Assumptions: (1) Gauss–Newton $G$ sets the top Hessian eigenvalues; (2) the surrogate holds for a few steps; (3) the residual $r=y-f$ (or its top-NTK projection) keeps its sign. For MSE, $\nabla^2_\theta\mathcal L=\underbrace{\tfrac1N\sum_i\nabla f_i\nabla f_i^{\top}}_{G}+\underbrace{\tfrac1N\sum_i(f_i-y_i)\nabla^2 f_i}_{S}$. Panels track $J$ on the eigenvectors of $H=\nabla^2_\theta\sum_i f$, the SLQ spectra, and how $H$'s eigenspaces rotate.

1 · Loss, sharpness, residual, and the spectral edges of $H$

Sharpness vs the EoS threshold $2/\eta$ (dashed). Residual is the signed $y-f(x)$ per output component. Last panel: the largest and smallest eigenvalue of the function Hessian $H$.

2 · Eigenvalue evolution — top-$n$

The $n$ largest eigenvalues over training of the function Hessian $H$, the loss Hessian ($=G+S$; its top eigenvalue is the sharpness), Gauss–Newton $G$, and the residual term $S$.

3 · Eigenvalue evolution — bottom-$n$

The $n$ smallest (most negative) eigenvalues of the same four matrices. ($G$ is PSD, so its bottom eigenvalues sit near 0.)

4 · $J$ projected onto eigenvectors of $H$

$\langle J, w\rangle$ onto the top-$n$ (1st) and bottom-$n$ (2nd) eigenvectors of $H$; then the same with $J$ normalized by its Frobenius norm, $\langle J/\lVert J\rVert, w\rangle$, onto the top-$n$ (3rd) and bottom-$n$ (4th) — the cosine alignment.

4b · $J\!\cdot\!r$ projected onto eigenvectors of $Q[u_1]$ (NTK-weighted function Hessian)

Multi-sample only. $Q[u_1]=\sum_a (u_1)_a\,\nabla^2 f_a$ — the function Hessian weighted by the top NTK eigenvector $u_1$; $J\!\cdot\!r=\sum_a r_a\nabla f_a$ — the Jacobian contracted with the residual. We eigendecompose $Q[u_1]$ and project $J\!\cdot\!r$ onto its top-$n$ (1st) / bottom-$n$ (2nd) eigenvectors; panels 3–4 normalize by $\lVert J\!\cdot\!r\rVert$.

Bottom row: first two — the top-$n$ / bottom-$n$ projections of $J\!\cdot\!r$ divided by $\lVert r\rVert$ (norm of the $d_n$-dim residual). Last two — alignment $\langle g_1, w_k\rangle$ between the top Gauss–Newton eigenvector $g_1=J u_1/\lVert J u_1\rVert$ (SVD-paired with $u_1$) and the top-$n$ / bottom-$n$ eigenvectors $w_k$ of $Q[u_1]$.

4c · $Q[u_1]\,(J\!\cdot\!r)$ projected onto Gauss–Newton singular vectors

Multi-sample only. No eigendecomposition of $Q[u_1]$ — instead apply it to $J\!\cdot\!r$ and project the resulting vector onto the top-$n$ singular vectors of the Gauss–Newton matrix $G$ (a scalar per direction; the 1st line is the largest GN singular vector). Left: raw; right: normalized by $\lVert Q[u_1](J\!\cdot\!r)\rVert$.

4d · Per-group $J$ and $J\!\cdot\!r$ — samples split by initial residual sign

Multi-sample only. At the start of training the samples are split into two fixed groups by the sign of their summed initial residual $\sum_c (y_{i,c}-f_{i,c})$ (sum over outputs when $d_{\text{out}}>1$). $H$ and $Q[u_1]$ — and hence their eigenvectors — are computed over all samples and are shared between the groups; only $J=\sum_{a\in\text{grp}}\nabla f_a$ and $J\!\cdot\!r=\sum_{a\in\text{grp}}r_a\nabla f_a$ change per group. Solid = positive-residual group, dotted = negative. First two: group $J$ onto the top-$n$ / bottom-$n$ eigvecs of $H$ (cf. §4). Last two: group $J\!\cdot\!r$ onto the top-$n$ / bottom-$n$ eigvecs of $Q[u_1]$ (cf. §4b).

9 · Predicted vs actual sharpness $\sigma_1$

Each window freezes $Q$ at $\theta_0$ and forward-predicts the top NTK eigenvalue $\sigma_1$ (reset to the measured value at every window start). Predicted (blue) vs actual (red). Col 1 — single sample, Eq. 13: $J_{t+1}=(I+\eta r_tQ)J_t,\ \sigma_1=\lVert J\rVert^2$. Col 2 — multi-sample, Eq. 21: iterate $J_{t+1}=J_t+\tfrac{\eta}{N}Q[J^\top r]$, read $\sigma_1=\lambda_{\max}(JJ^\top)$. Col 3 — multi-sample, Eq. 22: $\sigma_{1,t+1}=\sigma_{1,t}\big[1+2\eta\,(r\!\cdot\!u_1)\,v_1^\top Q[u_1]v_1\big]$, with $p_t=r\!\cdot\!u_1$ (the residual's projection onto the top NTK eigenvector — the $v_1$-coefficient of $J^\top r$) — the compact form. Col 4 — Eq. 23, the top-$|T|$-mode sum $\sigma_{1,t+1}=\sigma_{1,t}\big[1+2\eta\sum_{k\in T}\tfrac{\sqrt{\sigma_k}}{\sqrt{\sigma_1}}(r\!\cdot\!u_k)\,v_1^\top Q[u_1]v_k\big]$ with the bilinear $v_1^\top Q[u_1]v_k$ evaluated exactly (a frozen-$Q$ HVP). Col 5 — Eq. 29, the same sum but with $v_1^\top Q[u_1]v_k$ taken through the $\sum_{ij}\gamma_i\tau_{ij}(v_1^\top z_{ij})(v_v^\top z_{ij})(y_i^\top u_1)$ eigendecomposition of $Q$; the two columns differ by exactly that approximation. Sharpening when the bracket $>0$; sign-flipping residuals turn growth to decay — self-stabilization.

With the second-order PSD term. The recursions above keep only the first-order $\Delta\sigma_1$, which flips sign with the residual. Expanding $\sigma_1=u_1^\top(J+\Delta J)(J+\Delta J)^\top u_1$ leaves a dropped second-order term $\lVert\Delta J^\top u_1\rVert^2=(\eta/N)^2\lVert q_u\rVert^2\ge 0$ (single-sample: $\lVert\Delta J\rVert^2=(\eta r)^2\lVert QJ\rVert^2$) — always non-negative, a sharpening floor independent of residual sign. These panels add its running sum to each prediction: predicted + PSD (blue) vs actual (red).

7 · Multi-sample NTK & function-Hessian SVD

Shown only for multiple samples ($d_n=N\cdot d_{\text{out}}>1$). Same projections onto the eigenvectors of $M_2$ (reshape of the 2nd-topmost left singular vector): top-1, top-2, bottom-1, bottom-2.

8 · Jacobian onto right singular vectors of the $p\times(p\,d_n)$ function-Hessian reshape

Multi-sample only. The function-Hessian tensor reshaped as $\mathbb{R}^{p\times(p\,d_n)}$, $\;[\nabla^2 f_1\,|\,\cdots\,|\,\nabla^2 f_{d_n}]$; its right singular vectors ($p\,d_n$-dim) inner-producted with the flattened Jacobian $\mathrm{vec}(J)$. Left: true $J$. Right: $J$ normalized by its Frobenius norm $\lVert J\rVert_F$. (Heaviest panel — toggle off / raise eig every for large $d_n$.)

All eigenvalues/eigenvectors/densities are matrix-free via Lanczos (Hessian–vector products only) — no $p\times p$ matrix is formed (cap $p\le 100{,}000$). Use the Panels toggles (and G & S spectra) to switch off the costly analyses, and raise eig every, for large nets. SLQ refreshes at most ~50× per run. The 99%-energy subspaces use the top/bottom Ritz pairs (energy normalized by the captured part); $n$, $k$ clamped to $\lfloor p/2\rfloor$. The GPU backend (server.py) raises the cap to 10,000,000 and adds CIFAR-10 / sorting datasets and CNN / VGG11 / mini-GPT architectures.
